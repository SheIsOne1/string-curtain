The Journey

Bella is a meditation on memory and place — a portrait of a woman carrying the weight of inherited silence, learning to speak it into light. Shot across three continents over two years, the film follows a single thread of longing back to where it began.

Every story has a wound at its origin. For Bella, it was a photograph found in a drawer — a woman she had never met, wearing her own face. That image became the doorway through which the past could finally speak.

We wanted the camera to behave like memory itself — not documentary, not fiction, but something in between. Golden light. Long silences. Faces held in close-up until they reveal something they didn't intend to give.

"To tell a story honestly is to hold a candle up to the dark
and trust that someone, somewhere, recognises the room."
